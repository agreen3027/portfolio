My approach

"Good instructional design is invisible. The learner just understands — and can act."

I start with the people on the other side of the screen: what they need to know, what gets in their way, and what success actually looks like once the course or content goes live.

From there I run the work like a project — needs analysis, content development, stakeholder review, UAT, launch, and post-launch optimization — so nothing ships half-finished and nothing gets lost between teams.

Accessibility (ADA / WCAG) isn't a final checkbox in my process. It's a design constraint I build in from the first draft, because learning that excludes people isn't finished learning.
